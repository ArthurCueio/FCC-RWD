Nikola's Timeline

  • 1856: Born in the village Smiljan, in the Austrian Empire (present day Croatia)

  • 1870: Tesla moved far north to Karlovac to attend high school at the Higher Real Gymnasium. He finished a four-year term in three years, graduating in 1873

  • 1873: Returned to Smiljan. Shortly after he arrived, he contracted cholera, was bedridden for nine months and was near death multiple times.

  • 1875: Tesla enrolled at Austrian Polytechnic in Graz, Austria, on a Military Frontier scholarship. But never graduated

  • 1878: Tesla left Graz and severed all relations with his family to hide the fact that he dropped out of school.

  • 1881: Tesla moved to Budapest, Hungary, to work at a telegraph company, the Budapest Telephone Exchange. and and claimed to have perfected a telephone repeater or amplifier, which was never patented nor publicly described.

  • 1882: Tesla got another job in Paris with the Continental Edison Company. Tesla began working in what was then a brand new industry, installing indoor incandescent lighting citywide in the form of an electric power utility.

  • 1884: Tesla moved to the United States

  • 1887: Tesla developed an induction motor that ran on alternating current (AC), a power system format that was rapidly expanding in Europe and the United States

  • 1891: Tesla became a naturalized citizen of the United States. In the same year, he patented his Tesla coil.

  • 1893: Trying to come up with a better way to generate alternating current, Tesla developed a steam powered reciprocating electricity generator.

  • 1894: Tesla began investigating what he referred to as radiant energy of "invisible" kinds after he had noticed damaged film in his laboratory in previous experiments

  • 1898: Tesla demonstrated a boat that used a coherer-based radio control to the public during an electrical exhibition at Madison Square Garden. The crowd that witnessed the demonstration made outrageous claims about the workings of the boat, such as magic, telepathy, and being piloted by a trained monkey hidden inside.

  • 1890 - 1906: Tesla spent a great deal of his time and fortune on a series of projects trying to develop the transmission of electrical power without wires.

  • 1943: At the age of 86, Tesla died alone in Room 3327 of the New Yorker Hotel.
